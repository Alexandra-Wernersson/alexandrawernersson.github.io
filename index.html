Astronomy and Data Science

I am a Moore-Sloan Data Science Postdoctoral Fellow at the NYU Center for Data Science. Previously, I've been a PhD student at the Anton Pannekoek Institute for Astronomy at the University of Amsterdam, where I did my PhD thesis on magnetars with Anna Watts and Michiel van der Klis. I'm particularly interested in astrostatistics, i.e. the application of statistical methods in astronomy. I specialize in all those problems in time series analysis, where traditional (Fourier) methods come crashing down. I'm also interested in machine learning applied to problems in (X-ray) astronomy.
